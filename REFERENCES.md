## Bibliography 

### 1. Experiential Learning (Slide 2)
The slides explicitly reference David Kolb’s cycle of learning (Concrete Experience, Reflective Observation, Abstract Conceptualization, Active Experimentation).

*   Kolb, D. A. (1984). *Experiential learning: Experience as the source of learning and development*. Prentice-Hall.

### 2. The Mayer Model of Trust (Slide 5)
The definition of Reliability as "Ability, Integrity, and Benevolence" is a direct reference to the seminal paper on organizational trust by Mayer, Davis, and Schoorman.

*   Mayer, R. C., Davis, J. H., & Schoorman, F. D. (1995). An integrative model of organizational trust. *Academy of Management Review, 20*(3), 709–734. https://doi.org/10.5465/amr.1995.9508080335

### 3. Strategy & The Balanced Scorecard (Slide 6)
The concept of ensuring effort flows toward business value using a "Balanced Scorecard" refers to Kaplan and Norton's strategic management framework.

*   Kaplan, R. S., & Norton, D. P. (1992). The balanced scorecard: Measures that drive performance. *Harvard Business Review, 70*(1), 71–79.

### 4. Decision Making & Complexity (Slides 8-9)
The presentation explicitly cites the **Cynefin Framework** to explain the difference between "Edge" (Simple/Complicated) and "Core" (Complex/Chaotic) decision-making.

*   Snowden, D. J., & Boone, M. E. (2007). A leader’s framework for decision making. *Harvard Business Review, 85*(11), 68–76.

### 5. Organizational Topology & Leadership Transitions (Slide 10)
The "Coaching Cascade" and the shift in value creation from "doing" (L1) to "organizing" (L2) to "architecting" (L4) aligns with the *Leadership Pipeline* model, which defines how work requirements change at different leadership levels.

*   Charan, R., Drotter, S., & Noel, J. (2011). *The leadership pipeline: How to build the leadership powered company* (2nd ed.). Jossey-Bass.

### 6. Crisis Management: Peacetime vs. Wartime (Slide 12)
The concept of "Mode Switching" between Consensus (Peace) and Command (War) is popularized in modern tech management by Ben Horowitz.

*   Horowitz, B. (2014). *The hard thing about hard things: Building a business when there are no easy answers*. Harper Business.

### 7. Psychological Safety & Error Management (Slide 14)
The discussion on "Debugging Human Systems," avoiding a blame culture, and the necessity of "logging level debug" (transparency) rests on the empirical work regarding Psychological Safety.

*   Edmondson, A. (1999). Psychological safety and learning behavior in work teams. *Administrative Science Quarterly, 44*(2), 350–383. https://doi.org/10.2307/2666999

### 8. Systems Thinking (Slide 4 & 15)
The overarching thesis that an organization is a "complex, distributed system" and leadership is "Systems Architecture" is best supported by Donella Meadows' foundational work on systems thinking.

*   Meadows, D. H. (2008). *Thinking in systems: A primer*. Chelsea Green Publishing.
